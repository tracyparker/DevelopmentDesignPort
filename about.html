About Me

Hi, I'm Susan "Tracy" Parker. Welcome to my website! Thanks for stopping by!

I'm a graduate of the Code Louisville program. I have completed the Front End (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, etc.) and Back End (PHP, SQL) tracks.

I'm the founder of an app startup called MixxThis. I wear hearing aids and crowded bars with live music can be a challenge for me communication wise. (When you do a lot of band photos, you end up in a lot of bars.) I came up with the idea for an app to address the problems I was having. I took my idea to a Startup Louisville Weekend (Spring 2015) and people loved it! It was that weekend that a team was formed and MixxThis was born. We are still in the development stages of the site but hope to launch soon. I've learned a lot from the experience so far.

I'm an artist and photographer. I studied in the Commercial Art program at JCTC as a Photography major. (All background images on this site are my work.) I apprenticed for 6yrs with world renowned Master Potter, Melvin Rowe. I do digital fractal art, mostly using a program called Mandelbulb 3D, but I sometimes manipulate my photos to create fractal art. I dabble in assemblage art from time to time also. Like many who live in the Kentuckiana area, I'm a tornado survivor. Riding out an F4 is one of those things that always stays with you, it becomes part of who you are. (April 3, 1974 - Hanover, IN.) Weather, storms, tornadoes, and Mother Nature in general, are often the subject of my art/design and photography. My work has been exhibited on both sides of the Ohio River, used in a music video, on websites, and featured in local and online publications.

I'm currently employed by the U.S. Census Bureau, at their National Processing Center (NPC) in Jeffersonville IN. I'm assigned to the Geography Branch, as a Geography Technician. I work with satellite and street level imagery, specialized spatial editing/mapping programs, and databases. I'm also a member of Federally Employed Women (FEW), Derby City Chapter.
